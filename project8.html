Typographic Interpretation

Three interactive sketches—WHO, KNOW, and TIME

Overview

This project interprets three short words through interactive typography and motion. Each piece is built in p5.js: the meaning of the word shapes the visual logic—faces for identity, a key and door for knowledge, and a clock with a timeline for time. Together, they treat type not only as letters but as a way to stage an idea you can touch and play with.

WHO

The word who asks who someone is. The sketch uses many different faces as a stand-in for multiple identities. When you click, each face opens into a more detailed view of the same person—like moving from a crowd to a close-up—and the interaction links the abstract question “who?” to concrete human faces.

KNOW

The word know is shown as discovery after access: a key unlocks a door, and once the door opens, you “know” something you could not see before. The background layers in text from H. P. Lovecraft’s The Call of Cthulhu, which speaks to how human knowledge brushes against a vast, indifferent world. The piece sets personal, almost mechanical revelation (open the door) beside literary ideas about the limits and danger of what we think we know.

TIME

The word time is expressed with a clock and a timeline. The clock marks the ordinary sense of passing minutes, while the timeline stretches that idea into a line you can read along. On the timeline you can see past, present, and future as related positions—so the flow of time is not only a spinning hand but a story laid out in order.
